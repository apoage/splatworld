First, a correction that changes what you'd build. Q-C's headline cue isn't a sign cue. The estimator. Per splat, take n = s_min axis, and from your env-SH compute the irradiance on each face, E₊ = ∫L(ω)max(0, n·ω)dω and E₋ likewise for −n. Then (Lambertian, 1/π folded out):
L₊ = ρ₊E₊ + τE₋     (views on the +n side)
L₋ = ρ₋E₋ + τE₊     (views on the −n side)
Same τ in both lines — that's reciprocity earning its keep. Note this needs only irradiance from your decompose, and irradiance is precisely what low-order SH nails (Ramamoorthi's 9-coefficient result: the cosine kernel is a low-pass, ~1% error from any lighting). So the usual objection — "our env-SH is too blurry to localize the sun" — doesn't bite. You don't need the sun localized. You need E₊ and E₋, and order-2 SH gives you those to within a percent for unoccluded splats.
Lambertian means all same-side views collapse to one measurement, so that's two equations, three unknowns — one DOF short. Close it in three tiers:

Tier A — balanced faces (E₊ ≈ E₋). τ cancels exactly in the difference: L₊ − L₋ ∝ ρ₊ − ρ₋. Sign readout is the paler side. No threshold, no τ, no prior, no calibration. Also yields τ and the (ρ_ad, ρ_ab) prototypes for this asset.
Tier B — both-side views, unbalanced. (L₊/E₊) − (L₋/E₋) = (ρ₊−ρ₋) + τ(E₋/E₊ − E₊/E₋). Pin τ from Tier A's population, subtract the bias, read the sign. Note the correction blows up as E₊/E₋ grows — so sun-dominated splats are exactly the ones needing τ accurate. That formula is the statement "the sun is the confound, not the cue," quantified.
Tier C — one-side views only. No comparison exists. Match the visible face's chromaticity against Tier A's prototypes — chromaticity because it's invariant to E, exposure, and cosine falloff, which is what you need when you can't form a ratio.

A bootstraps B bootstraps C, per asset, unsupervised. No species priors, no ground truth, no external calibration. Each tier is a closed-form per-splat least-squares — convex, independent, embarrassingly parallel.
